Section 2

Cloud, AI & Productivity

Courses that make your portfolio look modern, cloud-aware, and AI-assisted.

Microsoft
Azure Fundamentals (AZ-900)
Common starting point for Azure — covers compute, networking, storage, security, governance, and administration. If your portfolio is Power BI- and Microsoft-heavy, do this before AWS to establish cloud literacy in the same ecosystem.
🌐 Online exam 🔰 Fundamentals ☁️ Azure
Official page
AWS
AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner
AWS's foundational credential — a good starting point for those with no prior cloud experience. 90-minute, 65-question exam available online or at Pearson VUE. Use it to show you can speak both Azure and AWS fluently.
⏱ 90 min exam 🌐 Online or Pearson VUE 🔰 Foundational
Official page
Microsoft
Microsoft Copilot Immersion Experience
Quick 1-day business course showing how Microsoft 365 Copilot works across finance, HR, sales, operations, marketing, and IT. Includes prompt-crafting and use-case exercises. Document prompt patterns and capture before/after productivity examples.
📅 1 day 🤖 M365 Copilot 💼 Business-level
Course page
GitHub
Introduction to GitHub Copilot
Covers code suggestions, triggering Copilot, configuration, troubleshooting, and the differences among Individual, Business, and Enterprise plans. Easiest way to show AI-assisted coding without jumping straight into a hard AI engineering cert.
🌐 Free module 🤖 AI coding 💻 Dev productivity
Module page
Microsoft
Azure AI Fundamentals (AI-901 Beta)
Because AI-900 retires on 30 June 2026, the current formal Microsoft AI path is AI-901 beta. Covers AI workloads, ML on Azure, computer vision, NLP, and generative AI. The exam page references Microsoft Foundry and Python code examples calling AI services.
🌐 Online exam ⚠️ AI-900 retires Jun 2026 🤖 Generative AI · NLP
AI-901 exam page
💡 Recommendation — Section 2
Treat Copilot Immersion + GitHub Copilot as your practical AI-tools productivity layer. Treat AI-901 as the formal certificate if you want a Microsoft AI credential on your profile. Do AZ-900 before AWS to stay in the Microsoft ecosystem first.

Section 5

Capstone Credentials

Credentials that give your portfolio long-term market credibility — choose based on your end goal.

FRM
Financial Risk Manager — GARP
Best if your end-game is risk management, derivatives risk, market risk, credit risk, or bank risk roles. GARP describes FRM as proof that you can assess, measure, and monitor risk in real-world situations. Two-part exam covering quantitative analysis, financial markets, valuation, risk models, and case studies.
✅ Choose FRM for: risk / market risk / derivatives risk roles
GARP FRM page
CFA Program
Chartered Financial Analyst — CFA Institute
Best if you want broader investment credibility. Curriculum spans quantitative methods, financial statement analysis, fixed income, derivatives, and portfolio management across three exams. The Learning Ecosystem provides practice questions, mock exams, and flashcards. Choose CFA for research, portfolio management, or asset management.
✅ Choose CFA for: investment research / portfolio management / asset management
CFA curriculum page
CQF
Certificate in Quantitative Finance
Most quant-native capstone credential. Online and part-time over six months with optional primers, six core modules, two electives, three exams, and a final project. Current elective menu includes algorithmic trading, advanced volatility modelling, and generative AI / LLMs for quant finance.
✅ Choose CQF for: quant trading / market making / serious quant-finance brand
CQF programme page

Section 6

What to Publish in Your Portfolio

A strong portfolio page shows: the problem, the data, the method, the result, the limitation, and the next step.

1
Executive KPI Dashboard
Based on PL-300 and the HKMA Power BI Storytelling course. Publish one live Power BI report with a written narrative.
2
Finance Data Pipeline Notebook
Use Python for cleaning, wrangling, and financial data preparation. Ideal output from the HKU SPACE Financial Data Analytics course.
3
Management Reporting Pack
A board-style PDF or slide deck with charts and a written narrative. Ideal combination of PL-300 and HKMA storytelling skills.
4
Valuation Workbook + Investment Memo
Combine Excel modelling, Python, sensitivity analysis, and Monte Carlo valuation. Derived from the HKU SPACE Financial Analysis course.
5
Cloud Architecture Diagram
Show how your analytics stack would run on both Azure and AWS. Covers AZ-900 and AWS Cloud Practitioner concepts in a real design.
6
Copilot Prompt Library
Document prompt patterns for analysis, summarisation, reporting, and coding. Combine Copilot Immersion and GitHub Copilot outputs.
7
Risk Model Notebook
Include VaR/CVaR, volatility modelling (GARCH), stress testing, and backtesting. Core output of the Financial Econometrics and Risk Modelling course.
8
Strategy Repository
Include transaction costs, slippage, execution logic, and performance attribution. Best sourced from Computational Finance and Quantitative Investing.
9
AI / ML Finance Demo
A sentiment model, anomaly detector, or simple predictive model. Ideal output from the AI and Deep Learning in Quantitative Finance programme.
10
Crypto / DeFi Research Note
Only if you want to signal specialisation in virtual assets and algorithmic trading. Sourced from the DeFi, Virtual Assets and Algo Trading module.
